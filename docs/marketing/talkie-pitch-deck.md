# Talkie Pitch Deck Outline

## Slide 1: Cover

Talkie

Private voice capture for people who think in motion

Subline:

Capture anywhere. Recover the full thread later. Turn speech into usable output on Mac.

## Slide 2: The Problem

The best ideas show up in motion.

They arrive:

- between meetings
- while coding
- during a walk
- right after a call
- in the middle of switching tasks

Most tools fail at that moment. They either add friction, lose context, or trap the
idea in a dead-end format.

## Slide 3: Why Current Tools Fall Short

Voice memos:

- easy to record
- hard to recover
- weak connection to the rest of work

Dictation tools:

- good for getting words onto a screen
- weak at preserving the thread around the thought

Cloud AI tools:

- strong at transformation
- weak on ownership and continuity

## Slide 4: The Thesis

Talkie is a private voice capture system.

It is built around one loop:

capture -> transcript -> context -> action

The breakthrough is not just transcription. It is continuity.

## Slide 5: How The Product Works

Capture:

- iPhone
- Apple Watch
- Mac dictation

Recover:

- transcript
- app context
- search
- history

Act:

- summaries
- tasks
- cleaned-up notes
- files
- workflows

## Slide 6: Why Mac Matters

Mac is where the raw thought becomes useful work.

On Mac, Talkie can:

- dictate into any app
- search past captures
- run workflows
- connect to shell and CLI tools
- route output into files and systems you already use

This is what separates the product from a mobile voice memo app.

## Slide 7: Why It Wins

### Speed

Catch ideas with less friction than typing.

### Continuity

Keep the transcript and surrounding context intact.

### Ownership

Local-first storage, iCloud sync, on-device options, and bring-your-own-model paths.

### Extensibility

Workflows, shell commands, webhooks, and CLI access for advanced users.

## Slide 8: Trust Story

Talkie is not built around a company-owned thought database.

Key points:

- local SQLite library
- iCloud / CloudKit sync
- on-device transcription path
- API keys in Keychain
- external providers are opt-in

Privacy here is architectural, not cosmetic.

## Slide 9: Wedge And Expansion

Wedge:

- capture and dictation for Mac-centric knowledge workers

Expansion:

- richer transcript transformation
- deeper workflows
- CLI and agent orchestration
- stronger team and multi-surface use cases

The advanced layer grows from the same core loop. It does not require a second story.

## Slide 10: Who It Is For

- founders
- engineers
- product people
- researchers
- writers
- operators

Anyone whose best raw material starts as spoken thought and needs to become structured
work.

## Slide 11: Use Cases

Examples:

- capture a post-call debrief on iPhone, turn it into a task list on Mac
- dictate a bug report and turn it into a GitHub issue
- speak a feature idea on a walk, recover it later by context, and turn it into a spec
- use workflows to route transcripts into notes, docs, or automation tools

## Slide 12: Closing

Talkie gives people a way to think out loud without losing the thread.

It starts as capture.
It becomes memory, structure, and action.
It stays on your side.
