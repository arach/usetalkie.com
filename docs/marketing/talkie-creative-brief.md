# Talkie Creative Brief

## Assignment

Build a coherent narrative for Talkie that makes the product legible in one pass.
Right now the product is strong, but the story can split in too many directions:
dictation tool, mobile capture app, AI workflow layer, local-first privacy product,
or voice operating system. All of those are true. The job is to make them feel like
one thing.

## The Short Version

Talkie is a private voice capture system for people who think in motion.

It lets you catch thoughts on iPhone, Apple Watch, or Mac, keep the transcript and
context intact, and turn that material into usable output later on your Mac.

The product is not just "voice notes with AI." The differentiated idea is continuity:
capture anywhere, recover the full thread later, and act on it inside a system you
control.

## The Audience

Primary audience:

- Builders, operators, and knowledge workers who think faster than they can type
- People who already live across Mac, iPhone, terminal, docs, and AI tools
- Users who care about privacy, ownership, and tool composability

Secondary audience:

- Technical teams that want voice capture without handing raw thoughts to a hosted SaaS
- Power users who want workflows, CLI access, and bring-your-own-model flexibility

This is not a mass-market "personal productivity" product in its clearest form. It
gets sharper when it speaks to people with active, messy, unfinished work.

## The Core Problem

Good ideas rarely arrive when someone is sitting neatly at a desk with a blank doc open.
They show up while walking, between meetings, in the middle of coding, right after a
call, or while switching tasks. Most tools break the thread at exactly that moment.

Voice memos capture the audio but strand it.
Dictation gets words onto the screen but loses the larger trail.
Cloud AI tools can transform text, but they often become the place where the thought
goes to disappear.

People do not need another place to dump thoughts. They need a system that can catch a
thought quickly, keep the surrounding context, and make it useful later.

## The Product Truth

Talkie works best when described as one system with distinct roles:

- iPhone and Apple Watch are capture surfaces
- Mac is where capture becomes search, structure, and action
- Local storage, iCloud sync, and bring-your-own-model options create trust
- Workflows, CLI access, and agents extend the same system for advanced users

That is the cleanest product truth in the codebase and on the site.

## Positioning

### Category

Private voice capture and action system for Mac-centric knowledge work.

### Positioning Statement

For people who think in motion, Talkie is a private voice capture system that turns
spoken thoughts into usable output. Unlike voice memo apps, cloud note takers, or
generic AI wrappers, Talkie preserves the transcript, context, and ownership chain
from capture through action.

## Messaging Hierarchy

### Master line

Talkie is a private voice capture system for people who think in motion.

### Supporting line

Capture on iPhone, Watch, or Mac. Recover the full thread later. Turn speech into
search, drafts, tasks, and workflows on your Mac.

### Expanded version

Talkie helps you catch ideas before they vanish, keep the moment attached, and turn
that raw material into something useful without giving up ownership of your data or
your tools.

## Message Pillars

### 1. Capture before the idea mutates

Talkie lowers the cost of catching a thought. You can record on iPhone, tap in from
Watch, or dictate directly on Mac. The point is not to be polished. The point is to
not lose the thought.

### 2. Keep the thread intact

A transcript by itself is not enough. Talkie preserves timing, app context, project
clues, and the fact that this was part of a larger working moment. That makes later
search and recovery useful instead of cosmetic.

### 3. Turn raw speech into usable output

Once the thought is safe, Talkie can help restructure it into summaries, notes, files,
tasks, and workflows. This is where Mac becomes the action surface.

### 4. Keep ownership legible

Talkie is local-first. Data lives on your devices. Sync runs through your iCloud.
Transcription can stay on device. External providers are opt-in and use your keys.
That trust story is real and should be treated as a core feature.

### 5. Stay open for power users

CLI access, shell actions, workflows, and agentic paths matter, but they are the
advanced layer, not the front door. They should feel like extensions of the system,
not a second product.

## What We Should Not Say

- Do not describe Talkie as a general AI app
- Do not frame the product as "just dictation"
- Do not make mobile sound like the primary product if Mac is where action happens
- Do not oversell agents on the homepage before the basic product loop is understood
- Do not lean on vague productivity language like "unlock your potential"

## Tone

The tone should feel:

- precise
- opinionated
- calm
- trustworthy
- technical without turning cold

It should not feel:

- startup-hypey
- mystical about AI
- productivity-guru-ish
- overexplained
- cute for the sake of it

## Brand Tension

Talkie sits in a useful tension:

- intimate enough for unfinished thoughts
- rigorous enough for real work
- open enough for technical users
- simple enough to use in one gesture

That tension is what makes the product interesting. The copy should preserve it.

## Proof Points

These are the facts that make the story credible:

- local SQLite library on Mac
- iCloud / CloudKit sync
- on-device transcription path
- API keys stored in Keychain
- global hotkey dictation
- mobile capture on iPhone and Watch
- search across memos and dictations
- workflows with file, shell, webhook, email, calendar, clipboard, and notification steps
- CLI access to capture and transcript data

- headline

- capture / context / action loop
- ownership section
- CTA

### 2. Mac page

Job: show where Talkie becomes useful work.

Focus:

- dictation
- search
- transcript cleanup
- workflows
- CLI

### 3. Mobile page

Job: sell the role of mobile without pretending it is the whole story.

Focus:

- low-friction capture
- lightweight input surface
- sync back to Mac

### 4. Security page

Job: convert technical skepticism into trust.

Focus:

- local-first
- iCloud custody
- on-device transcription
- bring-your-own-model

### 5. Demo assets

Job: make the loop obvious in under a minute.

Ideal demo sequence:

1. capture on phone
2. show transcript on Mac
3. search or recover by context
4. run a workflow or transform
5. show output landing somewhere useful

## Campaign Concepts

### Campaign 1: Think in Motion

Headline territory:

- For people who think in motion
- Catch the thought before it mutates
- Your best ideas do not arrive at your desk

This is the broadest, most brand-defining campaign.

### Campaign 2: Capture Anywhere, Resolve on Mac

Headline territory:

- Catch it on the move. Finish it on Mac.
- Capture anywhere. Continue where the work happens.
- Voice on the go, structure at your desk

This is the clearest product campaign.

### Campaign 3: Private by Architecture

Headline territory:

- Your voice stays on your side
- Privacy is not a setting. It is the system.
- Keep the chain on your side

This is the trust campaign.

## Final Recommendation

If Talkie tries to be introduced as voice capture, dictation, AI workflows, agent
orchestration, mobile memoing, and privacy software all at once, the story will blur.

If Talkie is introduced as a private voice capture system that starts anywhere and
resolves into usable output on Mac, the rest of the product clicks into place.
