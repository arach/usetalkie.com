# Talkie Demo Script Pack

## Purpose

This pack gives Talkie one consistent demo narrative at three lengths:

- 30 seconds
- 45 seconds
- 2 minutes

It also defines the three best scenario lanes so future demos do not drift into
"random cool features."

## The Core Demo Spine

Every Talkie demo should follow the same basic arc:

1. a thought appears in motion
2. Talkie captures it quickly
3. the transcript and context survive
4. Mac turns the raw thought into usable output
5. the close reinforces ownership and continuity

That is the product truth. The demo should make that loop obvious.

## Recommended Scenario Lanes

### 1. Walk-to-Spec

This is the best default scenario.

Story:

A product or feature idea appears while someone is away from their desk. They catch
it on iPhone, then later open it on Mac, recover the transcript, and turn it into a
structured draft or launch checklist.

Why it works:

- cleanly shows mobile as capture
- cleanly shows Mac as action surface
- demonstrates continuity instead of just transcription
- works for founders, product people, engineers, and builders

### 2. Post-Call Debrief to Tasks

Story:

Right after a meeting or customer call, someone records a quick debrief while details
are still fresh. Later on Mac, Talkie turns that raw memo into a summary and next-step
list.

Why it works:

- easy to understand
- strong emotional truth
- shows capture plus transformation without needing heavy technical context

### 3. Dictation to GitHub Issue

Story:

An engineer notices a bug or implementation concern while already working on Mac.
They dictate the thought directly, then run a workflow that turns it into a more
structured issue, note, or task.

Why it works:

- strongest power-user story
- shows dictation, workflows, and CLI adjacency
- best for technical audiences

## Best Default Recommendation

If Talkie only gets one hero demo, it should be:

`Walk-to-Spec`

It explains the whole system in the smallest number of moves:

- capture on phone
- continuity back on Mac
- output on Mac

## Visual Priorities

Every version should show these product truths clearly:

- iPhone is fast to capture from
- Mac is where the captured thought becomes useful work
- transcript and context are preserved
- privacy is structural, not a bolt-on slogan

Avoid:

- long feature tours
- too many UI modes in one cut
- making the advanced layer look like the whole product
- showing "AI magic" before the capture loop is understood

## 2 Minute Script

### Intent

A fuller founder, partner, or investor-style demo. This version explains category,
product loop, and trust without feeling like a feature dump.

### Voiceover

The problem with most capture tools is not that they fail to record something.
It's that they break the thread.

Ideas show up in motion. On a walk. Between meetings. Right after a call. In the
middle of coding. And by the time you're back at your desk, the shape of the thought
has already changed.

Talkie is built for that moment.

You can capture on iPhone, Apple Watch, or Mac, depending on what is cheapest in the
moment. The goal is not to be polished. The goal is to not lose the thought.

Once it's captured, Talkie keeps more than just the audio. It keeps the transcript and
the surrounding thread, so later recovery is actually useful.

Then, when you're back on Mac, that raw material becomes something you can work with.

You can search it. Clean it up. Pull out the important parts. Turn it into a draft,
task list, note, file, or workflow.

That is the real product idea:
capture anywhere, recover the full thread later, and act on it where the work happens.

And the trust story matters too.

Talkie is local-first. Your library lives on your devices. Sync can move through your
iCloud. External providers are optional. The product is designed so unfinished thoughts
do not have to disappear into someone else's system.

So if voice memos are good at capture but weak at action, and most AI tools are good
at transformation but weak at continuity, Talkie sits in the middle.

It gives you a private voice capture system that starts in motion and resolves into
usable output on Mac.

### On-Screen Beats

0-15s

- establish the problem with real work moments
- no desk, no clean doc, idea appears anyway

15-35s

- mobile capture sequence
- iPhone and optionally Watch

35-60s

- Mac transcript opens
- memo history, search, or context surfaces appear

60-90s

- transform the captured thought into something concrete
- summary, checklist, spec, task list, or output file

90-110s

- advanced layer if needed
- workflow, shell, CLI, or structured automation

110-120s

- trust/ownership close
- hero product shot and final line

### Best Final Line

Talkie lets you think out loud without losing the thread.

## Production Notes

### If you want one simplest shoot plan

Shoot this sequence:

1. capture a feature idea on iPhone while away from desk
2. open the same memo on Mac
3. search or recover it by transcript/context
4. turn it into a structured draft or checklist
5. end on Mac + iPhone hero shot

### If you want one simplest editing rule

Do not introduce a new feature unless it moves the main loop forward.

### If you want one simplest music rule

Keep it propulsive and clean, not cinematic or sentimental. The product should feel
fast, precise, and calm.
